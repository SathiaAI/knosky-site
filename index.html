What it is

A map of your work, not another file tree.

A file tree tells you what files exist. It doesn't tell you what you've actually built. KnoSky reads your repo or docs folder and renders the shape of it: folders become districts, files become buildings, every building links to the real, live file.

See it at a glance

Which areas are big, how they connect, where the gaps are. The whole project on one screen. New collaborators orient in minutes, not weeks.

Find anything in seconds

Search the city, or ask your AI “where does auth live / what did we decide about billing”, and jump straight to the live source.

Ground your AI

A local connector (MCP) lets Claude, Cursor, VS Code or Gemini answer from your source, with citations, instead of confidently making things up.

Why it's worth your minute

Comprehension, recall, and a grounded AI, at zero cost.

It's an index, not a copy. It runs entirely on your machine, stores pointers, never your file contents, and links back to the live source. Always fresh, $0 tokens to keep current, nothing uploaded.

Where it came from

Born from building Sathia.

A few weeks into building Sathia, an AI orchestration platform built on a simple idea (just say it, and intent becomes action), I realized I'd lost the map of my own project. Hundreds of decisions, specs, and architecture notes across a sprawling repo, and no way to see how it fit together.

So I built the thing I needed: point it at the repo, and the whole system becomes a city I can walk. KnoSky is that piece, pulled out and given away free.

Three steps, one minute

Node 18+, then:

node core/fs-indexer.mjs --root . --out city.json
node renderer/build-rich.mjs city.json city.html
# open city.html, add the MCP to your AI

Everything runs locally. Your code never leaves the machine.
